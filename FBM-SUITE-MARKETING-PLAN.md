## Part 2: Product-by-Product Marketing

### QuickPost (Homework Dictation)

**One-liner:** "Dictate tonight's homework to all your classes in 10 seconds."

**Pain it solves:** Teachers re-type the same homework announcement across 5-6 class sections every day. Canvas makes this tedious — navigate to each course, click Announcements, type, publish, repeat.

**Key selling angles:**
- "Open your phone, say it, done. All classes updated."
- "I used to spend 5 minutes posting homework. Now I spend 10 seconds."
- "Works from your phone between classes. No laptop needed."

**Content plan:**
| Format | Description | Channel |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| 30-sec screen recording | Side-by-side: posting homework the old way (5 min) vs QuickPost (10 sec) | Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts |
| 60-sec walkthrough | Full demo: dictate → review → post to all classes | YouTube, TpT listing |
| Text post | "I built this because I was tired of clicking through 6 classes to post the same homework" | Facebook groups, Reddit r/Teachers |
| Carousel | 4 slides: Pain → Solution → Demo screenshot → CTA | Instagram |

**Distribution:**
1. TpT listing (PDF setup guide as the "download")
2. Direct via Lemon Squeezy on foiledbymath.com/tools/quickpost
3. Canvas LMS Facebook group (87K members) — authentic story post
4. YouTube video embedded on product page

### PodLearn AI — With Teacher Use Case

**One-liner (personal):** "Type a topic. Get a researched podcast episode on your phone."

**One-liner (teacher use case):** "Create podcast episodes your students can listen to — on any topic you're teaching."

#### The Teacher Angle (New Positioning)

This is the unlock. PodLearn isn't just a personal learning tool — it's a **content creation tool for teachers**. Reframe it:

**"Make a podcast for your students."**

**Use cases for teachers:**
- **Review episodes:** "I made a 10-minute podcast reviewing everything we covered this unit. Students listen the night before the test."
- **Absent student catch-up:** "Missed class? Listen to the episode." Way more engaging than "read the notes."
- **Flipped classroom:** Students listen to the podcast as homework, class time is for practice and questions.
- **Differentiation:** Create different-depth episodes for different groups. Briefing for advanced, deep-dive for students who need more scaffolding.
- **Sub plans:** "Play this episode and answer the questions on the board." Instant meaningful sub plan.
- **IEP accommodation:** Audio content for students who struggle with text-heavy materials.

**Why teachers would pay:**
- They already spend hours making review materials. A podcast episode takes 3 minutes to request.
- Students actually *like* listening to podcasts. Way higher engagement than a worksheet review.
- It sounds professional — two voices, researched content, chapter markers.

**Key selling angles:**
- "Your students will actually listen to this. It's not a lecture recording — it's a real podcast."
- "Type 'review unit 5: solving equations' and get a 10-minute episode your students can listen to tonight."
- "Absent students? Send them the episode link. Done."
- "Sub plans that don't suck."

**Content plan:**
| Format | Description | Channel |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Full YouTube video | "I Made a Podcast for My Students (And They Actually Listened)" — show the workflow, play a clip, show student reactions | YouTube main channel |
| 60-sec demo | Type a topic → show the generated episode playing in a podcast app | Instagram Reels, Shorts |
| Sample episodes | Publish 3-4 sample math episodes publicly so teachers can hear the quality | Product page, TpT listing |
| Teacher testimonial | Andrew using it with his own classes — authentic, first-person | YouTube, social |
| Carousel | "5 Ways to Use AI Podcasts in Your Classroom" | Instagram |

**Pricing reframe for teachers:**
- Free tier (2 episodes/mo) is enough for teachers to try it
- $7/mo Plus tier = ~2 episodes per week = one per unit + extras
- Position as: "Less than a Starbucks a week to never make a review packet again"

## Part 4: Referral Program

### Structure

**"Share with a colleague. You both win."**

- Every paying customer gets a unique referral link (Lemon Squeezy affiliate feature)
- When a referred teacher signs up for any paid product:
  - **Referrer gets:** 1 month free (subscription) or $5 credit toward next purchase (one-time)
  - **Referee gets:** 20% off first purchase
- Referral link goes to the **hub page** (foiledbymath.com/tools) so the referred teacher sees the full suite

### Implementation
- Lemon Squeezy has built-in affiliate/referral tracking
- Add a "Share & Earn" section to each tool's settings page
- Include referral link in the post-purchase email (Day 3)
- Keep it to one tier — don't overcomplicate

### Why This Works for Teachers
- Teachers trust peer recommendations over ads (always have)
- Teachers share resources in department meetings, Facebook groups, and hallway conversations
- The referral reward is modest enough to feel authentic, not transactional
- The hub page lets the referred teacher choose the tool that fits *their* pain

## Part 5: Review & Social Proof Strategy

### Where Reviews Matter Most

1. **TpT** — reviews directly drive sales ranking and buyer trust
2. **Product pages** — testimonials on foiledbymath.com
3. **YouTube** — video testimonials from real teachers

### How to Get Reviews (Without Being Sleazy)

**Trigger-based in-app prompts:**
- Don't ask on Day 1. Wait for a usage milestone:
  - QuickPost: after 10th post
  - LessonBuilder: after 3rd generated lesson
  - Data Dashboard: after 5th login
  - PodLearn: after 5th episode
- Then show: "Enjoying [tool]? A quick review helps other teachers find it." with a direct link to TpT or a Google Form.

**Bonus resource incentive (TpT-safe):**
- "Leave a review → unlock a bonus [resource]"
  - QuickPost: "Canvas Quick Tips" PDF
  - LessonBuilder: 5 premium lesson templates
  - Data Dashboard: "Parent Conference Prep Checklist" PDF
  - PodLearn: 3 bonus episode credits
- This is a gift, not a bribe. TpT allows this when framed as "thank you for your feedback."

**Testimonial collection:**
- After Day 14 email, include a Google Form link: "Tell us how you use [tool] — we might feature you!"
- Offer to feature their classroom/name on the YouTube channel (teachers love being featured)
- Short video testimonials (selfie-style, 30 sec) — offer a free month of the bundle as a thank-you

### Social Proof Display

- Product pages: rotating testimonial carousel with teacher name, school type, and state
- TpT listings: screenshot the best reviews and add them to the preview images
- YouTube: compilation video "What Teachers Are Saying About FBM Tools" (once you have 5+)

### Bundle

| Bundle | Price | Savings vs Individual |
|--------|-------|-----------------------|
| FBM Teacher Bundle (monthly) | $15/mo | Includes PodLearn Plus + all one-time tools |
| FBM Teacher Bundle (annual) | $129/year ($10.75/mo) | Save 28% vs monthly |

**Bundle logic:** A teacher who buys QuickPost ($19) + Data Dashboard ($29) + LessonBuilder ($29) = $77 one-time. Adding PodLearn at $7/mo = $84/year + $77 up front. The bundle at $129/year is cheaper in Year 1 and *way* cheaper in Year 2+. It's an easy pitch.

**Upgrade path:** If a teacher already bought a one-time tool, offer bundle at a reduced rate for the first year (credit their one-time purchase).
